\# Life Expectancy vs GDP Per Capita



\## Overview

An exploratory data analysis examining whether wealth drives longer lives 

and how that relationship has evolved since 1950 (the average life expectancy being about 70 years globally and 1950's being 70 years ago.)



\## Question

Does money buy a longer life — and has that relationship changed over time?



\## Key Findings

\- GDP per capita has a strong positive relationship with life expectancy, 

&#x20; with the greatest gains occurring in the first $10,000 of wealth

\- Regional rankings of GDP and life expectancy are consistent — wealthier 

&#x20; regions live longer

\- Asia showed the steepest improvement in life expectancy since 1950, 

&#x20; preceding its economic boom

\- GDP and life expectancy are correlated but not purely causal — public 

&#x20; health, vaccines, and infrastructure play independent roles



\## Tools

\- Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn

\- Jupyter Notebook



\## Data Source

Our World in Data / World Bank — Life Expectancy vs GDP Per Capita dataset
